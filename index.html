Monophthongs

Single, steady vowel sounds.

Common monophthongs with examples
IPA Keyword Pattern notes Listen
/iː/ see Often written ee, ea, ie.
/ɪ/ sit Usually short i in closed syllables.
/æ/ cat Commonly written a before many consonants.
/ʌ/ cup Often represented by u, sometimes o (love).

Diphthongs

Gliding vowel sounds that move from one vowel quality to another.

Common diphthongs with examples
IPA Keyword Pattern notes Listen
/eɪ/ day Often shown by a-e, ay, ai.
/aɪ/ time Often shown by i-e, igh, y.
/oʊ/ go Commonly written o-e, oa, ow.
/aʊ/ house Usually written ou or ow.

Consonants

Consonants are grouped by voicing and place/manner of articulation.

Consonant examples and spelling patterns
IPA Keyword Pattern notes Listen
/θ/ think Usually spelled th (voiceless).
/ð/ this Also spelled th (voiced), especially function words.
/ʃ/ ship Often represented by sh, ti (nation).
/tʃ/ chair Often spelled ch, sometimes tch.
